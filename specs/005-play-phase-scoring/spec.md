# Feature Specification: Card Game 1000 — Play Phase, Scoring, Multi-Round & Victory

**Feature Branch**: `005-play-phase-scoring`
**Created**: 2026-05-15
**Status**: Draft
**Input**: User description: "Lobby, bidding and selling phases are done. need to implement gaming phase now. check docs/1000_Card_Game_Rules.txt"

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 — Card Exchange, Single Round of Trick Play, Round Summary (Priority: P1)

After bidding (and any selling) resolves, the round enters its gameplay phase instead of terminating at the "Round ready to play" handoff screen from feature 004. The declarer first exchanges 2 cards — selecting one card to give to the player on their left and another to give to the player on their right (each opponent receives one face-down card). After the exchange, each of the three players holds 8 cards. The declarer then leads the first trick. Play proceeds clockwise: each player plays exactly one card per trick, **must** follow the led suit if able, and otherwise plays any card. The highest card of the suit led wins the trick (no trump in this minimal slice). The winner of each trick leads the next. After all 8 tricks have been played, the system tallies each player's trick-card values (Ace 11, Ten 10, King 4, Queen 3, Jack 2, Nine 0) and shows every player an end-of-round summary that identifies whether the declarer made or missed their bid, lists each player's round score, and provides a single **Back to Lobby** button.

**Why this priority**: Without US1 there is no playable game — the bidding outcome from feature 004 has nowhere to go. US1 is the smallest end-to-end slice that closes the gameplay loop into a completable round: deal → bid → (sell) → exchange → 8 tricks → score → summary. Every later story (marriages, multi-round, special scoring) builds on this foundation.

**Independent Test**: From a state where bidding has just finalized a declarer at bid 100 (no marriages declared by anyone), the declarer is presented with their 10-card hand and prompted to pass one card to each opponent. After the exchange, each of the three players holds 8 cards. The declarer leads trick 1; each subsequent trick is led by the previous trick's winner. After trick 8, every client shows the round summary: nicknames, each player's trick points, the declarer's made/missed indicator, and a single **Back to Lobby** button (no other action controls).

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the declarer has just pressed Start the Game in the post-bid decision state (or has emerged as the new declarer after a successful sale), **When** the round transitions, **Then** all three clients enter the card-exchange phase within 1 second; the declarer's client shows the 10-card hand with a tap-to-select affordance and per-card "Pass to {leftOpponentNickname}" / "Pass to {rightOpponentNickname}" actions; the two opponents see "Waiting for {declarerNickname} to pass cards…".
2. **Given** the declarer has selected one card and tapped "Pass to {leftOpponentNickname}", **When** the server processes the action, **Then** that card is removed from the declarer's hand and added to the left opponent's hand (face-down to all viewers other than the receiver); the declarer's hand size becomes 9 and the left opponent's becomes 8; the declarer's client now restricts the remaining unsent card to "Pass to {rightOpponentNickname}" (no third option to send to the same opponent).
3. **Given** the declarer has passed both cards (one to each opponent), **When** the exchange completes, **Then** all three players hold exactly 8 cards each; the declarer's client transitions to the trick-play view with their full hand operable and a prompt "Lead trick 1 of 8"; the two opponents see "Waiting for {declarerNickname} to lead…".
4. **Given** the declarer leads trick 1 by tapping a card from their hand, **When** the server processes the action, **Then** that card appears face-up in the center for all three players; turn rotates clockwise; the next player sees their hand with only cards that **follow the led suit** as operable (if they hold at least one such card) — all other cards rendered as **disabled** for this trick.
5. **Given** the second-to-play player holds no cards of the led suit, **When** their turn arrives, **Then** every card in their hand is operable; whichever card they play does not win the trick unless it is of the led suit (no trump exists in US1).
6. **Given** all three players have played one card to a trick, **When** the trick resolves, **Then** the highest card of the led suit wins; the three cards animate into the winner's "tricks collected" stack (face-down to all viewers); the winner becomes the next-trick lead; the round's trick counter advances by 1.
7. **Given** trick 8 has just resolved, **When** the round ends, **Then** every client shows the round summary screen within 1 second: each player's nickname, each player's trick-card-point total (sum of card values from cards they collected), the declarer's bid, a clear made/missed indicator (e.g., "Declarer made the bid: +100" or "Declarer missed the bid: −100"), and each player's net round delta. A single **Back to Lobby** button is the only operable control.
8. **Given** the round summary is showing, **When** any player presses **Back to Lobby**, **Then** that player navigates individually to the lobby; the other two players remain on the summary until they each press their own button.

### User Story 2 — Marriages and Trump Declaration (Priority: P2)

A player who holds both the King and Queen of the same suit may declare a marriage during play by leading a trick with the K or Q of that suit on any of tricks 2 through 6 (inclusive), while holding at least 3 cards. Declaration is an explicit choice: the player taps the K or Q (the card is **tentative** at this point — not yet played), and a confirmation prompt offers "Declare marriage in {suit}?" (with the bonus value), "Play without declaring", or **Cancel** (back out and choose a different lead card). If they declare, the bonus (♣ 100 / ♠ 80 / ♥ 60 / ♦ 40) is immediately credited to their round total, the suit becomes the **trump** suit for that trick onward, and a visible marker on the table shows the current trump suit to all three players. Trump cards beat any non-trump card regardless of rank. A subsequent player (including the same player on a later lead) may declare a different suit as trump by playing K or Q of a different marriage, replacing the previous trump from that trick onward. Players may also choose to play a marriage's K or Q without declaring — the card is played as a regular card with no bonus and no trump change.

**Why this priority**: Marriages and trump are the strategic core of Thousand and the largest single source of round points (a single Clubs marriage is worth more than the minimum bid). The game is technically playable without them (US1), but the bidding economy collapses without marriage bonuses since most rounds end with the declarer missing their bid in no-trump. P2 adds the depth that makes the game itself work as a competitive contest.

**Independent Test**: From a round where the declarer holds K♥ and Q♥, the declarer leads trick 2 by tapping K♥. A prompt appears: "Declare marriage in Hearts (+60)?". They confirm. The status bar updates to show "Trump: ♥"; the declarer's round score is now +60 (before any trick points). On a later trick where the led suit is Diamonds and the declarer is out of Diamonds, they play 9♥ (a trump). 9♥ wins the trick over any non-trump card. After the round, the declarer's score includes the marriage bonus and their trick points.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a player is leading any of tricks 2 through 6 inclusive and holds at least 3 cards in their hand (including the card they would play) AND holds both K and Q of some suit, **When** they tap the K or Q of that suit, **Then** a confirmation prompt appears offering "Declare marriage in {suit} (+{bonus})?" with three options: **Declare and play**, **Play without declaring**, and **Cancel** (Cancel dismisses the prompt with no card played and returns the player to lead-card selection).
2. **Given** the prompt is showing, **When** the player confirms the marriage declaration, **Then** the marriage bonus is added to that player's round total immediately; the card is played face-up as the trick lead; the trump suit becomes the declared suit from this trick onward; the status display updates on all three clients to show the new trump suit; the marriage declaration is broadcast to all three clients with the player's nickname and suit (e.g., "{nickname} declared marriage in Hearts").
3. **Given** the prompt is showing, **When** the player chooses "Play without declaring", **Then** the K or Q is played as the lead card with no marriage bonus, no trump change, and no announcement.
4. **Given** a player is leading trick 1, OR leading trick 7 or 8 (and therefore holds fewer than 3 cards), OR is not leading the trick, **When** they tap their K or Q of a marriage suit, **Then** no declaration prompt appears; the card plays as a regular card.
5. **Given** trump has been declared as Hearts, **When** a player cannot follow the led non-trump suit, **Then** their client renders only their **trump cards** (Hearts) as operable; if they hold no trump either, every remaining card becomes operable. The server enforces this rule symmetrically: a non-following non-trump play when the player holds at least one trump is rejected with a user-visible reason.
6. **Given** trump has been declared, **When** a trick resolves, **Then** the winner is the highest **trump** played if any trump was played; otherwise the highest card of the suit led wins.
7. **Given** trump is currently Hearts and a different player on a later trick leads K♣ and confirms "Declare marriage in Clubs (+100)", **When** that trick begins, **Then** trump becomes Clubs from this trick onward; the previous Hearts trump is no longer in effect for subsequent tricks; the declaring player's round total gains 100.
8. **Given** the same player declares two marriages in one round (e.g., Spades on trick 2 and Hearts on trick 4), **When** the round ends, **Then** both bonuses stack: that player's round total includes both bonus values (80 + 60 = 140 in this example) in addition to their trick points.
9. **Given** a player holds K♥ but not Q♥, **When** they lead with K♥ on trick 3, **Then** no declaration prompt appears (no marriage is held); the card plays as a regular card.
10. **Given** the round ends with at least one marriage declared by any player, **When** the round summary appears, **Then** each player's row shows their trick points and their declared-marriage bonus broken out separately (e.g., "Trick points: 45 / Marriage bonus: 60 / Round total: 105"); the declarer's made/missed comparison uses their round total (tricks + marriages).

### User Story 3 — Multi-Round Play, Dealer Rotation, and Victory at 1000+ (Priority: P3)

The game does not end after a single round — it continues for multiple rounds until one player reaches a cumulative score of 1000 points or more. After each round's summary, every player presses **Continue to Next Round** (replacing the single Back to Lobby button from US1). When all three have continued, the dealer rotates clockwise (the previous round's P1 — the player clockwise-left of the dealer — becomes the new dealer), and a new round begins from the dealing animation (per feature 004 FR-002). Cumulative scores carry across rounds and may go negative. After scoring each round, the system checks if any player has reached 1000+: if so, the game ends immediately. Every client transitions to a final results screen showing every player's final cumulative score in descending order, the winner highlighted, and a **Back to Lobby** button. Each player navigates to the lobby individually.

**Why this priority**: Multi-round flow is what makes Thousand a *game* rather than a single-round demo. Without it, every "game" lasts one round and the bidding minimum of 100 (and the rules around barrels, multi-round penalties, etc.) lose all meaning. P3 turns the implementation from a round simulator into a complete game with a clear victory condition.

**Independent Test**: Three players play through a sequence of rounds. After each round summary, all three press **Continue**; a new round begins with the dealer rotated clockwise. Cumulative scores are visible on each round summary and on the persistent status display. When any player's cumulative score reaches 1000+, the next round does not begin; instead, all three clients show the final results screen with the winner highlighted and a Back to Lobby button as the only operable control.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the round summary screen is showing and no player has yet reached 1000, **When** any player views the screen, **Then** the only operable controls are **Continue to Next Round** and the persistent status area is updated to phase `Round complete` with each player's cumulative score visible.
2. **Given** all three players have pressed **Continue to Next Round**, **When** the server processes the third Continue, **Then** the dealer rotates clockwise (previous P1 becomes new Dealer; previous P2 becomes new P1; previous Dealer becomes new P2); a new round begins from the deal animation per feature 004 FR-002; bidding, selling, and play proceed normally; cumulative scores carry over.
3. **Given** all three players are mid-round in a game where cumulative scores include negative values (e.g., a player who has lost two bids in a row at large amounts), **When** any player looks at the persistent status area, **Then** the cumulative scores display each player's exact total including negative values (no flooring at zero).
4. **Given** the round ends with at least one player's new cumulative score ≥ 1000, **When** the system applies scoring, **Then** the game ends immediately: no further round begins; every client transitions to the final-results screen within 1 second of the score being applied.
5. **Given** the final-results screen appears, **When** any player views it, **Then** they see every player's nickname and final cumulative score in descending order; the player(s) with the highest score (and ≥ 1000) are clearly marked as the winner; a single **Back to Lobby** button is the only operable control.
6. **Given** two players' cumulative scores both reach ≥ 1000 in the same round, **When** the game ends, **Then** the player with the higher final score wins; on an exact tie at 1000, the **declarer of that round** wins (per the rulebook tiebreak — they were the one who triggered the victory by making their bid).
7. **Given** the final-results screen is showing, **When** any player presses **Back to Lobby**, **Then** that player navigates individually to the lobby; the other two players remain on the screen until they each press their own button.
8. **Given** the game is in a between-rounds state (round summary showing) and a player disconnects, **When** their grace period expires before they reconnect, **Then** the game is aborted: every remaining client sees a "Game aborted — {nickname} did not reconnect" screen with a single Back to Lobby button (consistent with feature 004 FR-021).

### User Story 4 — Special Scoring: Barrel (880–1000) and Three Consecutive Zeros (Priority: P4)

Two special scoring rules constrain late-game and habitual-passivity play. **(a) Barrel**: when a player's cumulative score enters the range [880, 1000) — including reaching it for the first time mid-game — they are placed "on the barrel". While on the barrel, the player must bid at least 120 to win any bid (they may still pass freely). A counter advances by 1 after each round while they remain on the barrel. If they reach 1000+ in any of those rounds, they win normally. If they complete 3 on-barrel rounds without reaching 1000+, a 120-point penalty is applied immediately (their cumulative score drops by 120) and they exit the barrel. If their score drops below 880 before the counter reaches 3 (e.g., they bid and missed), they exit the barrel and the counter resets — re-entering the barrel later restarts the counter at 0. **(b) Three Consecutive Zeros**: if a player ends 3 consecutive rounds with a round score of exactly 0 (no trick points, no marriages declared), a 120-point penalty is applied immediately and the counter resets.

**Why this priority**: These rules are core to the rulebook but only fire under specific late-game conditions; many full games complete without ever triggering them. They are P4 because they are independent of the main play loop and can be added on top of US3 without disturbing it. They are required for rule-correct play but are the lowest-impact features for a first playable version.

**Independent Test**: Drive a player's cumulative score to 895 via earlier rounds; the system marks them as "on barrel" on the next round's status display. During bidding, the system rejects bids below 120 from that player (with a user-visible reason). After 3 on-barrel rounds without reaching 1000, a 120-point penalty is applied to their score and the on-barrel marker is removed. Separately, drive a player to 3 consecutive rounds with a round score of exactly 0; on the third such round, a 120-point penalty is applied immediately.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a player's cumulative score becomes ≥ 880 and < 1000 after a round (or starts the game at that range, which cannot happen in v1 but is included for forward compatibility), **When** the next round starts, **Then** the status display shows an "On barrel" indicator next to that player's name with their current barrel-round counter (e.g., "On barrel — round 1 of 3").
2. **Given** a barrel player is bidding, **When** they submit a bid below 120 (e.g., 100 or 115), **Then** the server rejects the bid with a user-visible reason ("Barrel players must bid at least 120") and the turn does not advance. The barrel player may instead Pass freely.
3. **Given** a barrel player is in selling (the original declarer or an opponent buying), **When** they submit a bid below 120, **Then** the server rejects it with the same user-visible reason.
4. **Given** all three players pass on the opening 100 minimum and the dealer is on barrel, **When** the system applies the auto-declarer rule (feature 004 FR-011), **Then** the dealer becomes declarer at **120** (not 100) — the barrel minimum overrides the standard auto-bid of 100.
5. **Given** a barrel player completes a round and their cumulative score is still in [880, 1000), **When** the round ends, **Then** their barrel counter advances by 1; if it reaches 3 without their score reaching 1000+, a 120-point penalty is applied to their cumulative score immediately, the barrel indicator is removed, and the counter resets to 0.
6. **Given** a barrel player completes a round and their cumulative score has dropped below 880 (e.g., they bid 130 and missed, losing 130), **When** the round ends, **Then** they exit the barrel; their counter resets to 0; if they later re-enter the barrel, the counter starts fresh at 0.
7. **Given** a barrel player wins their bid and their cumulative score reaches 1000+ during the round (mid-trick or end-of-round scoring), **When** the system applies the round's scoring, **Then** they win the game (per US3 AS-4); the barrel counter is moot at that point.
8. **Given** a player ends a round with a round score of exactly 0 (no trick points and no marriages declared), **When** the round summary is computed, **Then** the system advances that player's "consecutive zeros" counter by 1. If the counter reaches 3, the system applies a 120-point penalty to that player's cumulative score immediately, the counter resets to 0, and the round summary shows a clear "Zero-round penalty: −120" entry for that player.
9. **Given** a player ends a round with a round score > 0 (any trick points or any marriage), **When** the round summary is computed, **Then** that player's "consecutive zeros" counter resets to 0.
10. **Given** the round summary is computed and both special-scoring rules trigger for the same player in the same round (e.g., third barrel-round AND third consecutive zero), **When** the score is applied, **Then** both penalties apply — the 120-point penalty for failing the barrel AND the 120-point penalty for three zeros (separately, so the player's cumulative score drops by 240 total) and both counters reset.

### Edge Cases

- **A player tries to play a card out of suit while holding the led suit**: server rejects the action with a user-visible reason ("You must follow suit with {suit}") and the turn does not advance. The client SHOULD pre-disable invalid cards to make this rejection unreachable under normal use, but the server is the source of truth.
- **A player tries to play a non-trump card when out of the led suit but holding trump**: server rejects with a user-visible reason ("You must play trump"). Same client-side pre-disabling expectation.
- **A player tries to declare a marriage they don't actually hold**: server rejects (the player must hold both K and Q of the declared suit at the moment of declaration); this can only arise from a tampered client.
- **A player tries to declare a marriage on trick 1, trick 7, or trick 8**: server rejects (per the timing rules); cannot arise from a properly-built client.
- **A player tries to declare a marriage when not leading a trick**: server rejects; cannot arise from a properly-built client.
- **The declarer tries to pass both card-exchange cards to the same opponent**: blocked by the client (the per-card destination becomes restricted after the first pass — see US1 AS-2) and re-validated by the server.
- **A barrel player wins the bid at exactly 120 and exactly makes it**: their cumulative score rises by 120; if that puts them at 1000+ they win the game; otherwise they remain on barrel (assuming they're still ≥ 880) and their counter advances normally.
- **A barrel player's counter reaches 3 in the same round they would have won the bid but missed**: they lose their bid amount AND incur the 120-point barrel penalty (both apply in the same round-end scoring). Both deltas appear in the round summary as separate line items.
- **Two players both on barrel simultaneously**: each player has their own independent counter; the barrel rules apply to each independently. Their bid-floor is 120 each.
- **A player on barrel passes the entire round (never wins the bid)**: their counter still advances by 1 per the rulebook (the round counts toward their 3-round window regardless of outcome).
- **A player ends a round with round-points = 0 but won a trick of three 9-value cards (Nine + Nine + Nine = 0)**: the consecutive-zeros counter still advances (the round score is exactly 0). The rulebook explicitly states this counts as a zero round.
- **A player disconnects mid-trick**: feature 004 FR-021 grace period applies symmetrically to trick play — if the disconnected player is the active one (about to play a card or about to lead), the trick pauses; if not, play continues. Grace expiry aborts the round (and the game terminates if a multi-round game was in progress).
- **A player disconnects during the card-exchange step**: if the disconnected player is the declarer (the active player in this step), the exchange pauses. If a non-declarer disconnects, the exchange continues; the declarer can still pass cards to either opponent's seat — passed cards await in that opponent's hand for them to see on reconnect.
- **All cards in a trick are non-trump and of different suits, with two of them not matching the led suit**: the only card of the led suit wins (the other two cards lose regardless of rank).
- **Server restart mid-round or mid-game**: in-memory game state is lost; all three clients are dropped (consistent with feature 004's existing posture). Multi-round games do not survive server restart in v1.
- **A 4th player tries to join a game already in-progress (mid-round, mid-game, or on the round summary screen)**: blocked — the game is not in `waiting` status and is not in the public lobby (feature 004 FR-020 applies to all phases of US1–US4).
- **A player tries to play a card from their hand after the trick they would lead has already resolved (race condition)**: server rejects the action (the card is no longer in their hand or it is no longer their turn); the client's pre-disabling and animation gates SHOULD prevent this from being rendered under normal use.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

- **FR-001**: When the round transitions out of feature 004's post-bid decision state via Start the Game, the server MUST emit a transition into the **card-exchange phase** instead of the "Round ready to play — next phase coming soon" handoff. The post-launch supersession is part of this feature — feature 004's FR-019 terminal screen is no longer reached during normal play (it only appeared because the play phase was unimplemented). All other feature 004 invariants — declarer/bid identification, hand size at handoff (declarer = 10), opponents = 7, talon empty — remain preconditions to the card-exchange phase.
- **FR-002**: During the card-exchange phase, the declarer MUST select exactly 2 cards from their 10-card hand: one card to give to the player on their left and one card to give to the player on their right. The declarer's client MUST present the 10 cards as tap-to-select affordances; tapping a card surfaces two destination buttons — `Pass to {leftOpponentNickname}` and `Pass to {rightOpponentNickname}` — and tapping a destination commits the pass for that card. **Each committed pass is final**: once the declarer taps a destination for a card, that card animates to the recipient immediately and the declarer cannot retract it. The card-selection affordance for the *second* card (which card to pass next) MAY be revisited freely until its destination is tapped — but once committed, the second pass is also final. After the first pass commits, the remaining unsent card's destination affordance MUST be restricted to the *other* opponent only (the destination already used MUST be hidden for the second card). There is no batch-Confirm step and no per-pass Undo.
- **FR-003**: The server MUST verify each card-exchange pass: (a) the card is currently in the declarer's hand at the moment of submission, (b) the destination is one of the two non-declarer seats, (c) the destination has not already received a card from this exchange. Violations MUST be rejected with a user-visible reason (per feature 004 FR-026) and MUST NOT mutate state.
- **FR-004**: Each passed card MUST be animated face-down on the receiver's client (the opponent sees a card-back travel from the declarer's hand area to their own hand area). The passed card MUST be animated **face-up** for the declarer for the entire motion (the declarer is allowed to remember which of their cards left their hand) and **face-down** on the third player's client (the spectator never learns the identity of a passed card). The receiver MUST see the card flip to face-up at the destination and learn its identity at that moment. Identities MUST be sent only to the recipient (and remain known to the declarer) — never to the third opponent (per feature 004 FR-022's minimum-knowledge rule).
- **FR-005**: After the card-exchange phase completes (both passes confirmed and animated), every player MUST hold exactly 8 cards. The phase MUST transition into **trick play** with the declarer prompted to lead trick 1.
- **FR-006**: The round MUST consist of exactly **8 tricks**. Each trick MUST collect exactly one card per player (3 cards total per trick). The declarer leads trick 1; for every subsequent trick the **winner of the previous trick** leads. The active player MUST be reflected in the persistent status display per feature 004 FR-025 (extended with trick-play phases below in FR-018).
- **FR-007**: On every trick, after the lead card is played, each subsequent player (in clockwise order) MUST be subject to the **follow-suit rule**: if the player holds at least one card of the led suit, they MUST play a card of that suit. If trump has been declared and the player cannot follow the led suit, they MUST play a trump card if they hold any. Only if they hold neither the led suit nor a trump may they play any card. The client MUST pre-disable cards that are not legal for the current play context; the server MUST re-validate every submitted card play and reject illegal plays with a user-visible reason (per feature 004 FR-026).
- **FR-008**: The winner of each trick MUST be determined as follows: if any trump card was played in the trick, the **highest trump** wins; otherwise the **highest card of the led suit** wins. Rank ordering within a suit is 9 < J < Q < K < 10 < A — the **Ten outranks the King and the Queen**, which is a non-obvious rule from the rulebook (the Ten is worth 10 points and is the second-highest card in the suit, behind only the Ace). The three played cards MUST animate face-up to the winner's tricks-collected area and then flip to face-down at the destination, per feature 004 FR-024's animation-and-visibility convention. Once a played card is in the collected stack, no client retains its identity (the server is the sole source of truth for trick history). Each player's tricks-collected area MUST be rendered on every client throughout trick play as a small **face-down stack of cards next to that player's seat**, visibly growing by 3 cards each time that player wins a trick, with a count badge "× N" overlaid showing the number of tricks that player has won so far (N ∈ {0..8}). The stack and badge appear for all three players (own seat plus both opponents); they reveal no card identities, only the trick count.
- **FR-009**: A player who is **leading any of tricks 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6** AND who **holds both the King and Queen of some suit** AND who **taps their K or Q of that suit** MUST be offered a **declare-marriage prompt** by their client. The tapped K or Q MUST be treated as **tentative** while the prompt is showing — it has not yet been played to the trick, and the client MUST NOT submit any `playCard` message to the server until the player resolves the prompt. The prompt MUST show the suit name, the bonus value (♣ 100, ♠ 80, ♥ 60, ♦ 40), and three choices: **Declare and play**, **Play without declaring**, and **Cancel**. **Cancel** MUST dismiss the prompt and return the player to the lead-card-selection state with no card played and no state change on the server (the player may then tap a different card, or re-tap the same K or Q to re-open the prompt). On trick 1, on trick 7, and on trick 8, the prompt MUST NOT be offered (the player holds fewer than 3 cards when leading trick 7 or 8, and the rulebook forbids declaration on trick 1); the K or Q plays directly. On any non-leading turn, the prompt MUST NOT be offered.
- **FR-010**: If the player chooses **Declare and play** on the marriage prompt, the server MUST: (a) re-verify the player holds both K and Q of the declared suit at the moment of submission; (b) re-verify the trick number is in [2, 6] and the player is leading; (c) add the marriage bonus to that player's **round score** (not cumulative — round only) and broadcast the declaration to all three clients (player nickname + suit + bonus); (d) set the trump suit to the declared suit, replacing any previous trump, from this trick onward (inclusive); (e) play the K or Q as the lead card normally. Any violation of (a) or (b) MUST be rejected with a user-visible reason.
- **FR-011**: If the player chooses **Play without declaring**, the K or Q MUST be played as the lead card with no marriage bonus, no trump change, and no public announcement. After this play the player no longer holds *both* K and Q of that suit, so future declaration of that specific marriage is impossible.
- **FR-012**: Multiple marriages declared by the same player in one round MUST stack — each adds its full bonus value to the player's round score. Trump can be replaced any number of times within a round by successive marriage declarations; the **most recently declared** trump is the one in effect for the current and subsequent tricks (until the next declaration or until the round ends). All three clients MUST display the current trump suit in the persistent status area at all times once trump has been declared.
- **FR-013**: After all 8 tricks have resolved, the server MUST compute each player's **round score** as: (sum of card values of all cards they collected from won tricks) + (sum of marriage bonuses they declared in this round). Card values: Ace 11, Ten 10, King 4, Queen 3, Jack 2, Nine 0. Marriage bonuses: Clubs 100, Spades 80, Hearts 60, Diamonds 40.
- **FR-014**: The server MUST compute each player's **round delta** to be applied to their cumulative score, using these rules:
  - **For the declarer**: if their round score ≥ their winning bid amount, the delta is **+bid** (they gain exactly the bid amount, not the round score); otherwise the delta is **−bid** (they lose the bid amount). The declarer's actual trick + marriage round score does **not** add to or subtract from their cumulative score beyond this made/missed determination.
  - **For each opponent (non-declarer)**: the delta is **+round score** (each opponent gains their full trick points plus any marriage bonuses they declared).
  - Plus any **special-scoring penalties** that fired this round (US4 — barrel three-round expiry: −120; three consecutive zeros: −120), applied additively to the relevant player's delta.
- **FR-015**: The server MUST surface a **round summary** to every client immediately after FR-013 and FR-014 compute. The summary view-model MUST include, for each player: nickname, seat, round trick points, round marriage bonus total (broken out separately), round delta (+/−), new cumulative score, and any applied penalties (barrel/three-zeros) shown as separate line items. The summary MUST also identify the declarer and their bid, and clearly indicate whether the declarer made the bid (round score ≥ bid) or missed it (round score < bid). The summary screen's operable control depends on game state:
  - If no player has reached 1000+ AND multi-round is in scope (US3 implemented): the operable control is **Continue to Next Round** on each player's client (a single click per player; the next round starts when all 3 have continued).
  - If at least one player has reached 1000+ OR multi-round is not yet in scope (US1 standalone): the operable control is **Back to Lobby**, navigated individually per feature 004's FR-019 convention.
- **FR-016**: When all 3 players have pressed **Continue to Next Round** on the round summary (US3), the server MUST: (a) rotate the dealer one seat clockwise (the seat clockwise-left of the previous dealer becomes the new dealer); (b) start a new round by restarting the deal sequence per feature 004 FR-002 with the new seat assignments; (c) preserve every player's cumulative score, barrel state, and consecutive-zeros counter across the round boundary. Cumulative score may be negative.
- **FR-017**: After every round's scoring, the server MUST check for the **victory condition**: if any player's new cumulative score is ≥ 1000, the game ends immediately. No new round begins; the server transitions all three clients to the **final-results phase**. The final-results view-model MUST include:
  - **Per-player summary**: every player's nickname, seat, final cumulative score (in descending order), and a clear winner indicator on the winning row.
  - **Per-round history table**: an ordered list of all rounds played in the game. For each round the table MUST contain the round number, the declarer's nickname, the declarer's bid, and — for each of the three players — that player's **round delta** (e.g., `+120`, `−145`, `+35`) and their **running cumulative total** after the round was applied. Any special-scoring penalties that fired in a given round (barrel −120 / three-zeros −120) MUST be visible as a separate line item or annotation on that round's row for the affected player.
  - The history table MAY be rendered as a scrollable area on small screens; the implementation chooses how to fit it. The view-model MUST contain the data; layout is implementation-decided.
  
  If exactly one player is at ≥ 1000, they are the winner. If multiple players are at ≥ 1000 in the same round, the **highest-score player** wins. If two or more players are tied at the top score (whether at exactly 1000 or any higher value), the tiebreak is: (a) if the **declarer of the triggering round** is among the tied players, the declarer wins; (b) otherwise, fall back to **seat order** — P1 (clockwise-left of the dealer) beats P2 (clockwise-right of the dealer) beats the Dealer. The final-results screen's only operable control on each client is **Back to Lobby**; pressing it returns that player individually to the lobby (consistent with feature 004 FR-019 / FR-021 navigation convention).
- **FR-018**: The persistent status display from feature 004 FR-025 MUST be extended to cover the new phases introduced by this feature. The display MUST show:
  - **Phase**: one of `Card exchange | Trick play | Round complete | Game over` in addition to the existing phases from feature 004.
  - **Active player**: the declarer during card exchange; the trick leader / next-to-play during trick play; null during Round complete and Game over.
  - **Trick number**: "Trick {N} of 8" during trick play; absent in other phases.
  - **Current trump suit**: shown once trump has been declared and persists until replaced by another marriage declaration or the round ends. Before any trump declaration, the indicator shows "No trump".
  - **Cumulative scores**: each player's cumulative game score, visible in the persistent status display **at all times** from the moment the game screen first appears through Game over — every phase of every round, including the round-1 dealing animation and round-1 bidding (where every player's cumulative is 0). During card exchange, bidding, selling, declarer decision, and trick play of rounds 2+, the display shows the running cumulative totals carried over from the previous round summary. The display MUST update on all three clients within 1 second of any round-end score application (consistent with feature 004 FR-025's status-update timing) so post-summary totals are reflected the moment the next round begins.
  - **Barrel status** (US4): for any player whose cumulative score is in [880, 1000), an "On barrel — round N of 3" marker MUST appear next to their seat label.
  - All other elements from feature 004 FR-025 (disconnected-players indicator, viewer's-turn framing) remain in effect.
- **FR-019**: The minimum-knowledge rule from feature 004 FR-022 MUST extend to the new card movements introduced by this feature, specifically:
  - During card exchange, a passed card's identity is sent only to (a) the recipient when the pass animation completes and (b) the declarer throughout. The third opponent never learns the identity. After the round ends, the server stops resending any of these identities (per feature 004 FR-023).
  - During trick play, each played card's identity is sent face-up to all three clients (the card is currently in the center). Once the trick resolves and the three cards move to the winner's collected stack, every client MUST stop displaying those identities and the server MUST NOT resend them on subsequent state updates. The collected-stack cards animate face-up during the motion and flip to face-down at the destination (per feature 004 FR-024's pattern for cards leaving viewers' visible scope).
  - At round end, every player's full collected-stack contents are revealed in the round summary view-model for that player's row (their own collected cards) — but each viewer sees only their own collected cards' identities, never the other two players' (a viewer can verify their own trick points, but cannot audit the others'). The server's round-score totals (per FR-013) are broadcast to all three clients; the source cards are not.
- **FR-020**: The action-gating rule from feature 004 FR-026 MUST extend to the new phases. Concretely:
  - **Card exchange**: only the declarer's client shows operable card-tap and destination-button affordances; opponents see neither (**hidden** for opponents per the FR-026 "permanently locked out for this phase" rule).
  - **Trick play**: on each player's turn, only that player's client shows operable cards; the cards that are legal to play (per FR-007's follow-suit rule) are operable, the rest are **disabled** (eligible-but-not-legal: the player will lead or play later in the round). On the other two players' clients during the same turn, **all** cards in their hand are **disabled** (eligible-but-waiting); they will become operable when the turn rotates to them.
  - **Marriage declaration**: only the player who has just tapped their K or Q to lead (when conditions hold) sees the declaration prompt; the other two players see no prompt (**hidden** — they cannot declare for someone else).
  - **Round summary** (between rounds, US3): only **Continue to Next Round** is operable; all in-round controls are **hidden**.
  - **Final results** (US3): only **Back to Lobby** is operable; all other controls are **hidden**.
- **FR-021**: The server MUST track **per-player barrel state** persistently across rounds for the duration of the game. The state MUST include: an `onBarrel` boolean (true when the player's cumulative score is in [880, 1000)) and a `barrelRoundsUsed` counter (integer in {0, 1, 2}, advanced at each round end while the player is on barrel). Entering the barrel for the first time resets `barrelRoundsUsed` to 0. Dropping below 880 mid-game resets both `onBarrel` (false) and `barrelRoundsUsed` (0). Reaching 1000+ at any point ends the game per FR-017 (the barrel state is moot at that point).
- **FR-022**: While a player is on barrel, the server MUST enforce a **bid floor of 120** for that player in both main bidding (feature 004 FR-008) and selling-bidding (feature 004 FR-015). Concretely:
  - The server MUST reject any bid submission from a barrel player with `amount < 120`, with a user-visible reason: "Players on barrel must bid at least 120."
  - The barrel player's client MUST initialize the bid input to `max(smallestLegalBid, 120)` at the moment it appears for them (extending feature 004 FR-028's initialization rule). The stepper MUST clamp its range to `[max(smallestLegalBid, 120), 300]`.
  - **Pass remains always operable** for a barrel player (consistent with feature 004 FR-028's separate Pass button).
  - If all 3 players pass on the opening 100 (feature 004 FR-011's auto-declarer rule) AND the dealer is on barrel, the auto-declared bid MUST be **120** (not 100). The status display MUST reflect this.
- **FR-023**: After every round's scoring, the server MUST advance each on-barrel player's `barrelRoundsUsed` counter by 1. If, after the advance, the counter has reached 3 AND the player's cumulative score is still in [880, 1000), the server MUST apply a **−120 penalty** to that player's cumulative score, reset `barrelRoundsUsed` to 0, and update `onBarrel` based on the post-penalty score (typically false, but if the post-penalty score is still ≥ 880 — possible if they were at 999 — they remain on barrel and the counter restarts at 0). The penalty MUST be shown as a separate line item on the round summary ("Barrel penalty: −120").
- **FR-024**: The server MUST track **per-player consecutive-zero state** across rounds: a `consecutiveZeros` counter (integer in {0, 1, 2}). After every round's scoring, for each player: if their **round score** (trick points + declared marriage bonuses for the round, BEFORE FR-014's made/missed adjustment for the declarer) is exactly 0, advance the counter by 1; otherwise reset the counter to 0. If the counter reaches 3, apply a **−120 penalty** to that player's cumulative score, reset the counter to 0, and show "Zero-round penalty: −120" as a separate line item on the round summary.
- **FR-025**: The card-exchange step, trick play, and round-summary phases MUST all honor feature 004 FR-021's grace-period and disconnect handling. Specifically:
  - If the active player in any of these phases disconnects, the active turn MUST pause and the server MUST reject state-changing actions until that player reconnects or their grace period expires.
  - If a non-active player disconnects, the round MUST continue and the remaining live players' actions on their own turns MUST still be accepted.
  - On the round-summary screen (between rounds, US3): a player's **Continue to Next Round** press is **sticky** — once the server records it, the press persists across a subsequent disconnect by that same player. If a player has not yet pressed Continue and disconnects, the next round cannot begin until they reconnect or grace expires. If a player has pressed Continue and then disconnects, their press still counts toward FR-016's "all 3 pressed" condition; the next round begins as soon as the other two press, **provided the disconnected player is still within their grace window** at the moment of the third press (they then rehydrate into the new round per FR-026). The grace timer continues running across the press; if it expires before the other two press, the game is aborted via `game_aborted` regardless of the recorded Continue.
  - On the final-results screen (US3), disconnects are no-ops: the game has already ended; the disconnected player simply does not navigate back to the lobby until they reconnect.
  - Grace-period expiry mid-round MUST broadcast `round_aborted` per feature 004 FR-021; grace-period expiry on the round-summary screen MUST broadcast a new `game_aborted` event (with `disconnectedNickname` and `reason: 'player_grace_expired'`) that transitions all remaining clients to a **Game aborted** screen — a single Back to Lobby button per the existing pattern.
- **FR-026**: The reconnect-rehydration rule from feature 004 FR-027 MUST cover the new round states. On reconnect within grace:
  - **Card-exchange phase**: send the reconnecting player a state snapshot including their hand (per FR-019 above and feature 004 FR-022's minimum-knowledge rule), whether they are the declarer, the current pass-counter ("0 of 2 cards passed" / "1 of 2 cards passed"), and the persistent status view-model.
  - **Trick-play phase**: send the reconnecting player a state snapshot including their current hand identities, the current trick number, the current trump suit (if any), the cards currently in the center of the table (the in-progress trick), the active player, the cumulative scores, and the persistent status view-model. Do NOT send card identities for any tricks already collected (the round-end summary will reveal only that player's own collected cards per FR-019).
  - **Round-summary phase**: send the full summary view-model (it is broadcast to all three clients and contains no card identities the player isn't allowed to see).
  - **Final-results phase**: send the final-results view-model.
- **FR-027**: The per-player soft rate limit from feature 004 FR-030 MUST extend to all new state-changing actions introduced by this feature: card-exchange `pass` submissions, trick-play `playCard` submissions, marriage-declaration confirmations, and `continueToNextRound` clicks. At most one such message per player per 250 ms is processed; excess is silently dropped server-side with no broadcast or acknowledgement.
- **FR-028**: The rejection feedback surface from feature 004 FR-031 MUST also surface every new user-visible rejection reason introduced by this feature (e.g., "You must follow suit", "You must play trump", "Barrel players must bid at least 120", invalid card-pass submissions, invalid marriage declarations) as a non-blocking toast on the rejecting player's client only, never broadcast. Silently-throttled drops produce no toast.
- **FR-029**: The game-record cleanup rule from feature 004 FR-032 is **superseded** by this feature for multi-round games. The server MUST retain the game record across rounds — cumulative scores, barrel state, consecutive-zeros state, dealer rotation, and player-seat assignments persist as long as the game is in progress. The game record MUST be purged from the in-memory store at the moment the game ends:
  - When the system broadcasts the **final-results** transition (FR-017) — purge after broadcast; the post-game screen is purely client-side.
  - When the system broadcasts **`round_aborted`** (feature 004 FR-021, grace-period expiry mid-round) — purge after broadcast.
  - When the system broadcasts **`game_aborted`** (FR-025, grace-period expiry between rounds) — purge after broadcast.
  - In all three cases, a player who disconnects after the broadcast and reconnects later MUST be routed directly to the lobby (the game record no longer exists; FR-026 only applies while the game is still in progress).
- **FR-030**: Every visible card movement introduced by this feature (declarer's hand → opponent on card exchange; player's hand → center on trick play; center → winner's collected stack on trick resolve) MUST be presented as an animated motion on every client (consistent with feature 004 FR-024). State-changing actions MUST NOT be accepted by the client until the corresponding animation has completed (e.g., the next trick's lead-prompt is not operable until the previous trick's collect-animation has finished). Cards that a viewer is not authorized to see during a motion MUST animate as card-backs per the existing pattern.

### Key Entities

- **Round**: extended from feature 004's `Round` to include trick-play state. Adds: `phase` now includes `card-exchange | trick-play | round-summary`; `trickNumber` (1–8 during trick play); `currentTrickLeader` (seat); `cardsInCurrentTrick` (array of {seat, card}); `currentTrumpSuit` (one of {♥, ♦, ♣, ♠} or null); `declaredMarriages` (list of {playerId, suit, bonus} entries for this round); `collectedTricks` (per-player count of tricks won, plus the server-only list of cards collected by each player for end-of-round scoring); `roundScores` (per-player trick-points + marriages, computed at round end).
- **Game**: a new persistent-across-rounds entity. Tracks: `cumulativeScores` (per-player integer, may be negative); `dealerSeat` (rotates clockwise each round); `currentRoundNumber` (integer, starts at 1, increments per round); `barrelState` (per-player {onBarrel, barrelRoundsUsed}); `consecutiveZeros` (per-player counter); `gameStatus` ∈ `in-progress | game-over | aborted`. The Game entity persists across rounds and is purged only at game-end (per FR-029).
- **Trick**: a single 3-card collection within a round. Attributes: `trickNumber` (1–8); `leaderSeat`; `cards` (an ordered list of {seat, card}); `winnerSeat` (determined after all 3 cards play, per FR-008); `cardsCollectedFaceDown` (the trick's 3 cards held server-side; revealed only to the winner via the round-summary's own-collected-cards row).
- **Marriage Declaration**: an immutable entry created when a player chooses "Declare and play" on the marriage prompt. Fields: `playerId`, `suit`, `bonus` (40 / 60 / 80 / 100), `trickNumber` (2–6). Stored on the Round and broadcast to all three clients. Cannot be retracted.
- **Round Summary**: a view-model emitted to all three clients at round end. Per-player rows containing: nickname, seat, trick points, marriage bonus total, round delta, new cumulative score, applied penalties (barrel: −120 / three-zeros: −120). Plus: declarer identity, declarer bid, declarer made/missed indicator. The summary holds no card identities except for each viewer's own collected-trick cards (FR-019).
- **Final Results**: a view-model emitted to all three clients when the game ends. Contains (a) the per-player summary (nickname, seat, final cumulative score, winner indicator) and (b) the per-round history table — an ordered list of every round played, each row carrying round number, declarer nickname, declarer bid, and per-player {round delta, running cumulative, applied penalty annotations}. To support this, the server MUST retain a **round-history log** for the duration of the game: at every round-end (when FR-013/FR-014 compute the deltas), the server appends one history entry per round to an in-memory list keyed by the game record. The log is broadcast as part of the final-results message and is purged with the game record per FR-029.

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

### Measurable Outcomes

- **SC-001**: After bidding (and any selling) resolves and the declarer presses Start the Game, the card-exchange phase appears on all three clients within 1 second; the declarer's hand is operable for selection within the same window. *(Verified manually via the `quickstart.md` 3-tab walkthrough; the implementation MUST broadcast the phase transition synchronously from the Start-the-Game handler.)*
- **SC-002**: Across 100 played rounds (single-round, no marriages, no trump), every trick collects exactly 3 cards; every round contains exactly 8 tricks; every player ends each round with exactly 8 cards played; the per-trick winner is always the highest card of the led suit. 100% correctness.
- **SC-003**: 100% of out-of-suit plays when the player holds at least one card of the led suit are rejected by the server with a user-visible reason; 100% of non-trump plays when the player is out of the led suit and holds at least one trump card (after trump is declared) are rejected with a user-visible reason. The client's pre-disabling makes these rejections unreachable in normal use, but the server enforces them as the source of truth.
- **SC-004**: 100% of declared-marriage submissions are accepted only when (a) the player holds both K and Q of the declared suit at submission time, (b) the trick number is in [2, 6], and (c) the player is leading. Any violation is rejected with a user-visible reason. Across 100 played rounds, the displayed trump suit on every client always matches the most recent valid declaration (or "No trump" if none has been declared).
- **SC-005**: At round end, the computed round score for every player exactly equals the sum of the card values in their collected tricks plus the sum of their declared-marriage bonuses. The declarer's cumulative-score delta is exactly +bid if their round score ≥ bid and exactly −bid otherwise. Each opponent's cumulative-score delta is exactly their round score. Across 100 played rounds, 100% match between expected and applied deltas.
- **SC-006**: Across 100 multi-round games (US3), the dealer rotates clockwise exactly one seat per round; cumulative scores carry forward exactly (no drift, no rounding); the game ends in the same round the first player reaches 1000+ and the final-results screen appears within 1 second of the score being applied.
- **SC-007**: 100% of barrel-player bid submissions with amount < 120 are rejected with a user-visible reason; the barrel player's bid stepper clamps to [120, 300] on every appearance; after 3 on-barrel rounds without reaching 1000+, exactly −120 is applied to the barrel player's cumulative score and the barrel state resets correctly.
- **SC-008**: 100% of three-consecutive-zero events (a player ends 3 rounds in a row with round score exactly 0) trigger a −120 penalty applied immediately, with the counter resetting to 0 after the penalty.
- **SC-009**: Across all states in this feature, no client ever holds the identity of any card outside the viewer's currently-visible scope per feature 004 FR-022 — including: cards in opponents' hands at any point during card exchange and trick play; cards collected into a winner's tricks-collected stack (after the collect animation completes); cards collected by other players when the round summary is shown (each viewer sees only their own collected cards in the summary).
- **SC-010**: At every moment from the start of card exchange to the end of the round (or to the end of the game in US3), 100% of clients display the current phase, active player (when applicable), trick number (during trick play), current trump suit, cumulative scores, and barrel markers consistently; any server state change is reflected on all three clients within 1 second.
- **SC-011**: 100% of action attempts that violate phase-or-turn gating (out-of-turn play, non-declarer attempting card exchange, opponent attempting a marriage declaration, post-game-end action submission) are rejected by the server with a user-visible reason and do not change game state.

## Assumptions

- **Builds directly on feature 004**: this spec assumes feature 004 (lobby, dealing, bidding, selling, declarer decision) is fully implemented and shipped. The "Round ready to play — next phase coming soon" handoff screen from 004 FR-019 is superseded by the card-exchange transition introduced here (FR-001). Feature 004's FRs around minimum-knowledge (FR-022, FR-023), animations (FR-024), persistent status (FR-025), action gating (FR-026), reconnect snapshots (FR-027), bid input (FR-028), rate limiting (FR-030), and toast feedback (FR-031) all remain in force; this spec extends them rather than redefining them.
- **3-player mode only**: 4-player support remains a future feature. Dealer rotation clockwise applies within the 3-seat ring.
- **In-memory persistence**: cumulative scores, barrel state, and consecutive-zero counters live in the server's in-memory game store for the lifetime of the game and are lost on server restart (consistent with feature 004's posture). Cross-session game persistence is out of scope.
- **No turn timer**: trick-play turns are untimed in v1, consistent with feature 004's bidding-untimed posture. Disconnect handling uses feature 003's grace period.
- **"Rospisat'" / round-pass option is OUT of scope**: the rulebook glossary mentions an option for the declarer to pass the round without playing, giving opponents 60 points each. This variant is not implemented in v1.
- **Card exchange: declarer assigns each card to a specific opponent (left or right)**: the rules say "1 card to the player on their left, 1 card to the player on their right" but do not explicitly state whether the declarer chooses which card goes to which opponent or whether the server assigns automatically. The strategic richness of the game (especially around "pass weak cards" vs. "pass dangerous threats") makes the explicit-assignment interpretation the natural one. Adopted as the v1 behavior; this can be re-litigated if implementation friction emerges.
- **Single game ends at 1000+**: there is no "play another game" loop. After the final-results screen, players return individually to the lobby and create a new game if they want to play again.
- **Tie at 1000 exact in a simultaneous-victory round**: tiebreak is "declarer wins" (the player who made their bid that round). This matches the rulebook's GAME OVER SCENARIOS section.
- **Visual / styling details**: card-back artwork, animation easing and durations, summary screen layout, "On barrel" badge styling, and victory-screen celebration animations are decided at planning and implementation time, not in this spec. The spec enforces the **information** that must appear and the **animation-required** constraint (FR-030), not the precise visual treatment.
- **Accessibility / colorblind support remains out of scope**: deferred to a future user-settings feature, consistent with feature 004's stance.

## Clarifications

### Session 2026-05-15

- Q: When the marriage-declaration prompt appears (FR-009), is the tapped K/Q already committed to the trick, or can the player back out entirely? → A: Tapping K/Q is **tentative** — the prompt offers **Declare and play**, **Play without declaring**, AND **Cancel**. Cancel dismisses the prompt with no card played and returns the player to lead-card selection. FR-009, the US2 description, and US2 AS-1 were updated to encode this.
- Q: When are cumulative scores visible to all clients? → A: At **all times** from the moment the game screen first appears through Game over — every phase of every round, including the round-1 dealing animation and round-1 bidding (where every player's cumulative is 0). FR-018 was updated to encode this.
- Q: During card exchange, can the declarer undo a pass that has already been committed (animated to the recipient)? → A: **No** — each committed pass is final. The card animates to the recipient immediately on commit and cannot be retracted. The declarer can freely revisit the *selection* of the second card (which card from the remaining 9) before its destination is tapped, but once a destination is tapped, that pass also commits. No batch-Confirm step, no per-pass Undo. FR-002 was updated to encode this.
- Q: During trick play, what does each player see for tricks already collected by each of the three players? → A: A small **face-down stack of cards next to each player's seat** that grows by 3 cards each time that player wins a trick, with a count badge "× N" overlaid (N ∈ {0..8}). No identities revealed — only the visible stack and trick count. FR-008 was updated to encode this.
- Q: Does the final-results screen show only the final cumulative totals, or also a per-round history? → A: **Both** — final totals (per-player nickname, seat, final cumulative score in descending order, winner highlighted) PLUS a **per-round history table** listing every round's number, declarer, bid, and each player's round delta + running cumulative + any applied special-scoring penalty. The server retains a round-history log for the duration of the game and broadcasts it as part of the final-results message. FR-017 and the Final Results entity were updated to encode this.
- Q: How is the tiebreak resolved when multiple players reach ≥ 1000 in the same round and tie at the top score (at exactly 1000 OR at any higher tied value, e.g., two opponents both at 1050)? → A: **Declarer of the triggering round wins any tie among the top ≥1000 scorers**, regardless of the exact tied value. If the declarer is NOT among the tied players, fall back to **seat order**: P1 (clockwise-left of dealer) > P2 (clockwise-right of dealer) > Dealer. FR-017 was updated to encode this.
- Q: On the round-summary screen between rounds (US3), what happens if a player presses **Continue to Next Round** and then disconnects before the other two press? → A: **Sticky press, grace timer keeps running.** The press persists across the disconnect; the next round begins as soon as the other two press, *provided the disconnected player is still within their grace window* at the moment of the third press (they rehydrate into the new round per FR-026). If the disconnected player's grace expires before the other two press, the game aborts via `game_aborted` regardless of the recorded Continue. FR-025 was updated to encode this.
