## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 — Game Auto-Starts and Bidding Begins (Priority: P1)

The moment a waiting room reaches 3 players, the game starts automatically — no host has to press "start". All three players transition to the table screen, where the deal plays out as an animation: cards visibly fly out from a central deck one at a time, clockwise, into each player's hand area and the talon, until each player has 7 cards face-up in front of them, the other two players' hands are 7 cards face-down on the sides, and the 3 talon cards rest face-up in the center. Once the deal animation completes, the player to the dealer's left is prompted to either Bid (a number ≥ 100, multiple of 5, ≤ 300) or Pass; the other two see whose turn it is and a "Waiting…" indicator. Bidding rotates clockwise; once a player passes they are locked out of the round. Bidding ends when only one bidder remains (or all 3 passed → dealer is auto-declarer at 100).

**Why this priority**: Without this story there is no game. It establishes the auto-start trigger, the table layout, and the first interactive phase. It is the smallest end-to-end slice that turns a full lobby into a started game.

**Independent Test**: Three players join one waiting room. Within seconds all three browsers transition to the game screen and play a deal animation in which cards visibly travel from a central deck to each player's area and the talon, one at a time. After the animation completes, each player sees their own 7 cards, the talon's 3 cards face-up, and the leftmost-of-dealer player has live Bid/Pass controls. The bidding round resolves to a single declarer with a bid in [100, 300].

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a waiting room with 2 players, **When** a 3rd player joins, **Then** within 2 seconds all three clients transition to the game screen and begin a dealing animation in which cards visibly travel from a central deck to each player's area and the talon, one at a time, clockwise.
2. **Given** the deal animation is playing, **When** any player watches it, **Then** they see the 24-card deal proceed in the interleaved clockwise pattern from FR-002 — three opening rounds of P1 → P2 → P3 → Talon (4 cards each), followed by four rounds of P1 → P2 → P3 (3 cards each), for 24 cards total — with each card's motion clearly animated rather than appearing instantaneously, and with the same canonical order on every client. Cards dealt to opponents animate face-down (only the card-back is rendered, with no rank/suit) on the viewer's client; cards dealt to the viewer themselves animate face-up; the 3 talon cards animate face-up for everyone.
3. **Given** the deal animation has completed, **When** a player inspects their hand, **Then** they see exactly 7 cards face-up; opponents' hands are shown face-down with the correct count (7); the 3 talon cards are face-up in the center, visible to all three players. Bid/Pass controls do not appear on the first bidder's client until the animation has completed.
4. **Given** the bidding has begun, **When** the active bidder submits a bid of 100, **Then** the bid is accepted, the current high bid is 100, and the next player clockwise becomes the active bidder.
5. **Given** the active bidder submits a bid of 107, **When** the server validates it, **Then** the bid is rejected with a user-visible reason (not a multiple of 5) and the turn does not advance.
6. **Given** the active bidder submits a bid of 100 when the current high bid is already 100, **When** the server validates it, **Then** the bid is rejected (must be strictly higher) and the turn does not advance.
7. **Given** two players have passed and one is still bidding, **When** the bidding phase resolves, **Then** the still-bidding player becomes the declarer with their last bid.
8. **Given** all three players pass on the opening minimum, **When** the bidding phase resolves, **Then** the dealer (the game's host) is the declarer at exactly 100.
9. **Given** the game screen has appeared, **When** any player looks at the screen at any point during dealing and bidding, **Then** they see a persistent status area showing the current phase (`Dealing` or `Bidding`), whose turn it is (with their own turn distinguishable from an opponent's), the current high bid (or the opening minimum 100 before any bid is accepted), and which players — if any — have already passed.

### User Story 2 — Declarer Takes the Talon and Chooses to Sell or Start (Priority: P2)

After bidding completes, the declarer's hand grows from 7 to 10 by absorbing the 3 talon cards (which everyone has been able to see throughout bidding). The center talon area becomes empty. The declarer then sees two clearly-labeled buttons: **Sell the Bid** or **Start the Game**. Opponents see "Waiting for declarer to choose…" and the talon area is empty. If the declarer chooses Start, the round transitions to the next (out-of-scope) play phase — this spec only emits the handoff signal.

**Why this priority**: Establishes the declarer-decision state that gates the optional Selling phase. Without it, the declarer has no choice and selling is unreachable.

**Independent Test**: From a state where bidding has just ended, the declarer's client shows 10 cards in hand and exactly two action buttons (Sell, Start); both opponents' clients show "Waiting…" and an empty talon area. Pressing Start emits a single "round ready for play phase" event and the spec's scope ends.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** bidding just finalized a declarer, **When** the round transitions, **Then** the declarer's hand size becomes 10 and the talon area is empty for all clients within 1 second.
2. **Given** the declarer is in the post-bid decision state, **When** they view their controls, **Then** they see exactly two actions: Sell the Bid, and Start the Game (no others).
3. **Given** the declarer presses Start the Game, **When** the server processes the action, **Then** the round emits a "play phase ready" signal with the declarer ID and final bid amount, and all three clients show a "Round ready to play — next phase coming soon" message with a single operable **Back to Lobby** button (no other action controls); each player who presses the button returns individually to the lobby.
4. **Given** the declarer is in the post-bid decision state, **When** any opponent tries to perform a card action, **Then** the server rejects it (only the declarer can act in this state).
5. **Given** the round is in the post-bid decision state, **When** any player looks at the screen, **Then** the persistent status area shows phase `Declarer deciding`, identifies the declarer by nickname, shows the final winning bid, and frames the active turn as "Your turn" for the declarer's client and "Waiting for {declarer nickname}…" for the two opponents.

### User Story 3 — Selling the Bid (up to Three Attempts) (Priority: P3)

The declarer can attempt to sell their bid by selecting 3 cards from their 10-card hand and placing them face-up in the center. The two opponents then have a clockwise chance to "buy" — bid higher than the current bid (multiple of 5, ≤ 300) or pass. The original declarer cannot bid here. If one opponent buys, that opponent becomes the new declarer at the new bid, takes the 3 exposed cards (hand grows to 10), and the original declarer's hand shrinks back to 7; the Selling phase ends. If both opponents pass, the exposed cards return to the original declarer's hand and they may attempt again — up to 3 total attempts. Each retry must use a **different** set of 3 cards than the previous attempts. After 3 failed attempts, only Start the Game is available.

**Why this priority**: Optional refinement of the declarer phase; the game can finalize a declarer without it (Story 2 alone). Adds strategic depth but is a clear, isolated slice on top of Story 2.

**Independent Test**: From a state where the declarer has 10 cards and a bid of 120, the declarer selects 3 cards and clicks "Sell". Both opponents see the 3 face-up cards plus Bid/Pass controls. If opponent A bids 125 and opponent B passes, opponent A becomes the new declarer, holds 10 cards, has bid 125, and the original declarer holds 7 cards. Selling phase ends and the round emits the same "play phase ready" signal as Story 2.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the declarer is in the post-bid decision state, **When** they press Sell the Bid and choose 3 cards, **Then** those 3 cards appear face-up in the center to all three players and the declarer's hand size becomes 7.
2. **Given** Selling has begun with bid 120, **When** an opponent submits a bid of 125, **Then** the bid is accepted and they become the new high bidder.
3. **Given** Selling has begun, **When** the original declarer attempts to bid, **Then** the server rejects the attempt (original declarer is excluded from selling bids).
4. **Given** Selling has begun and one opponent has bid 125, **When** the other opponent passes, **Then** the buyer (the one who bid 125) becomes the new declarer, takes the 3 exposed cards into their hand (now 10), the original declarer's hand stays at 7, the bid is now 125, and the Selling phase ends.
5. **Given** Selling has begun, **When** both opponents pass without buying, **Then** the 3 exposed cards return to the original declarer's hand (back to 10) and the declarer is again in the post-bid decision state with their attempt counter incremented.
6. **Given** the declarer is on attempt 2 of selling, **When** they choose the same 3 cards as attempt 1, **Then** the server rejects the selection (must be a different set).
7. **Given** the declarer has used 3 selling attempts with no buyer, **When** they return to the decision state, **Then** the only available action is Start the Game (Sell the Bid is disabled).
8. **Given** Selling ends (either by sale or after 3 failed attempts), **When** the new declarer (or the original declarer after 3 fails) presses Start the Game, **Then** the round emits the "play phase ready" signal with the finalized declarer and bid.
9. **Given** the round is in the Selling phase, **When** any player looks at the screen, **Then** the persistent status area shows phase `Selling`, the current high bid, the active buyer's nickname (or "Your turn" for the active buyer), which non-declarer opponent has already passed (if any), and the current attempt counter as "Attempt N of 3".

### Edge Cases

- **A player disconnects during bidding or selling**: if the disconnected player is the one whose turn it is to act, the active turn pauses while their grace period (from feature 003) is active; if the disconnected player is *not* the active player, the round continues normally and the remaining live players may still act on their turns. In either case, every client MUST show a "Disconnected…" status next to the disconnected player's seat for the duration of their grace period. If the grace period expires, the round is aborted; the server broadcasts an `round_aborted` message and players return to the lobby. Detailed user-facing treatment of mid-game disconnects beyond the "Disconnected…" indicator (e.g., dedicated pause overlay, resume animation) is deferred to a future spec.
- **A player wants to leave mid-round**: there is no explicit "Leave Game" control rendered on the game screen during in-progress phases (Bidding, Declarer deciding, Selling). To exit, the player closes the browser tab (or navigates away), which is treated as a passive disconnect — the standard FR-021 grace-period flow applies. If they do not reconnect within the grace period, the round is aborted and the remaining players return to the lobby.
- **All three players pass on the opening minimum bid (100)**: the dealer (the game's host) becomes declarer at exactly 100 per the rulebook and proceeds straight to the post-bid decision state.
- **Declarer tries to expose the same 3 cards on a re-sell attempt**: blocked by the server with a user-visible message; declarer must change the selection.
- **Declarer tries to sell after 3 failed attempts**: server rejects; only Start the Game is permitted.
- **A bid is not a multiple of 5, not strictly greater than the current high bid, or exceeds 300**: server rejects with a user-visible reason; turn does not advance.
- **A 4th player tries to join a game already in-progress**: blocked — the game is no longer in `waiting` status and is not in the public lobby.
- **Server restart mid-round**: in-memory game state is lost; all three clients are dropped (acceptable for v1, consistent with the current architecture).
- **Multiple "Sell" or "Start" clicks from the declarer in rapid succession**: server processes the first and ignores duplicates (idempotent for this state transition).
- **Client attempts to inspect cards no longer in view** (e.g., opens dev tools to look at the JavaScript state, hoping to read the previously-exposed Selling cards after they have returned to the declarer's hand): the client state holds no identities for cards that are not currently visible to the viewer, so there is nothing to read. The server retains the full history but never re-sends it to that client.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

- **FR-001**: Server MUST auto-transition a `waiting` game to `in-progress` immediately upon the 3rd player joining; no host "start" button.
- **FR-002**: Server MUST shuffle a fresh 24-card deck (ranks {9, 10, J, Q, K, A} × suits {♥, ♦, ♣, ♠}) at round start and deal 7 cards to each of the 3 players plus 3 cards to the talon, one card at a time, following an **interleaved clockwise pattern**: rounds 1, 2, and 3 each deal one card to P1, then P2, then P3, then the talon (4 cards per round, 12 cards total — after which every player holds 3 cards and the talon holds 3); rounds 4 through 7 each deal one card to P1, then P2, then P3 only (3 cards per round, 12 more cards). Total 24 cards. P1 is the seat clockwise-left of the dealer (the first bidder per FR-004); P2 and P3 are the next two seats clockwise. The same canonical 24-step sequence MUST be the basis of the animation on every client so the visible deal order is identical across the table.
- **FR-003**: Server MUST assign the dealer as the game's host (the player who created the game). Multi-round rotation is out of scope for this spec.
- **FR-004**: Server MUST identify the first bidder as the seat clockwise-left of the dealer.
- **FR-005**: Each player's client MUST display the player's own hand face-up at the bottom of the table; opponents' hands face-down on the sides (showing card count only). The opponent who acts immediately **after** the viewer in clockwise turn order MUST appear on the viewer's **left**; the other opponent MUST appear on the viewer's **right**. This mapping MUST be applied consistently on every client so the visual seating reflects the clockwise rotation. The viewer's own hand MUST be auto-sorted **by suit (descending by trump/marriage value), then by rank within suit (ascending)**. The canonical suit order is **♣ (Clubs, 100) → ♠ (Spades, 80) → ♥ (Hearts, 60) → ♦ (Diamonds, 40)** — left to right in the hand, matching the marriage point values from the rulebook. Within each suit, ranks ascend: 9, 10, J, Q, K, A. The hand MUST be re-sorted using this same rule whenever its contents change (e.g., after the declarer absorbs the talon, or after exposed cards return to the declarer's hand on a failed sell). The viewer cannot manually reorder their hand.
- **FR-006**: The 3 talon cards MUST be displayed face-up in the center, visible to all 3 players, from deal time until the declarer absorbs them. Once absorbed, they become part of the declarer's private hand (face-down to opponents).
- **FR-007**: The active bidder's client MUST be the only one showing Bid/Pass controls; other clients show the turn indicator and a "Waiting…" state.
- **FR-008**: System MUST accept a bid only if all are true: integer multiple of 5, ≥ 100, > current high bid, ≤ 300. Otherwise rejected with a user-visible reason; turn does not advance.
- **FR-009**: Once a player passes, the system MUST exclude them from further bidding for the rest of the round.
- **FR-010**: When exactly one bidder remains (others have passed), the system MUST declare them the declarer with their last accepted bid.
- **FR-011**: If all 3 players pass on the opening 100, the dealer MUST become declarer at bid = 100.
- **FR-012**: When the declarer is determined, the system MUST transfer the 3 talon cards from the center into the declarer's hand (now 10 cards) and clear the talon area for all clients.
- **FR-013**: After taking the talon, the declarer's client MUST show exactly two actions: Sell the Bid, and Start the Game.
- **FR-014**: During Selling, the declarer MUST select exactly 3 distinct cards from their 10-card hand to expose face-up; all 3 clients see those cards.
- **FR-015**: During Selling, only the two non-declarer players may bid; bid validation rules per FR-008 apply, with the current high bid initialized to the original bid (so the first overbid must be at least original + 5). The **first opponent offered the buy** on each selling attempt MUST be the seat clockwise-left of the declarer (paralleling FR-004, which puts the first main bidder clockwise-left of the dealer); the other opponent goes second. Opponent bidding then rotates clockwise (starting with the not-yet-passed opponent whose turn it is) and continues among the non-passed opponents until exactly one opponent remains non-passed OR both opponents have passed — mirroring the main bidding loop in FR-009 and FR-010. Concretely: on each turn the active opponent either submits a valid bid (becoming the new high bidder) or passes (locked out of *this selling attempt only*; eligibility resets on the next attempt). Once one opponent has passed and the other has bid at least once, the bidder wins per FR-017; if both pass on their respective first turns without ever bidding, the attempt fails per FR-016.
- **FR-016**: During Selling, if both opponents pass, the 3 exposed cards MUST return to the declarer's hand (back to 10) and the declarer's attempt counter MUST increment. Cards on each retry MUST be a different set than each prior attempt within this round.
- **FR-017**: During Selling, if one opponent bids and the other passes (leaving exactly one non-passed opponent), that opponent MUST become the new declarer at the new bid, MUST take the 3 exposed cards into their hand (now 10), and the original declarer's hand MUST be restored to 7; the Selling phase ends.
- **FR-018**: The declarer MUST be limited to at most 3 selling attempts per round. After 3 failed attempts, the Sell the Bid action MUST be disabled — only Start the Game remains.
- **FR-019**: When the declarer presses Start the Game, the system MUST emit a single "play phase ready" event identifying the final declarer and final bid amount. Upon receiving this event, every client MUST display a "Round ready to play — next phase coming soon" message together with a single **Back to Lobby** button as the only operable control; pressing the button returns that player to the lobby (the other two players remain on the message screen until they each press their own button). The persistent status area continues to show phase `Round ready to play` and the finalized declarer + bid for the duration of this screen. (The play phase itself is out of scope for this spec.)
- **FR-020**: A game whose status is `in-progress` MUST NOT appear in the public lobby and MUST reject `join_game` attempts.
- **FR-021**: If a player disconnects mid-round, the server MUST behave as follows: (a) if the disconnected player is the one whose turn it is to act in the current phase (Bidding active bidder, Declarer deciding, Selling active buyer, or the declarer during the Selling card-selection step), the active turn MUST pause and the server MUST reject all state-changing actions from any client until that player reconnects or their grace period expires; (b) if the disconnected player is not the active player, the round MUST continue and the remaining live players' actions on their own turns MUST still be accepted. In both (a) and (b), every client MUST display a "Disconnected…" indicator next to the disconnected player's seat for the duration of their grace period, and MUST remove it the moment the player reconnects. If the grace period expires before reconnection, the round MUST be aborted with a `round_aborted` broadcast to remaining players, who are returned to the lobby.
- **FR-022**: **Minimum-knowledge rule.** The server is the sole authority on the full deck and on every card's true identity. A client MUST only ever receive the rank/suit of cards that are *currently* visible to that specific viewer at that specific moment. All other cards MUST be represented to that client as opaque card-backs (rank/suit unknown) accompanied only by structural facts (e.g., "opponent A has 7 cards"). Concretely, at minimum:
  - A player receives the identities of their own hand.
  - All three players receive the identities of the 3 talon cards from the moment of the deal until the declarer absorbs them.
  - All three players receive the identities of the 3 cards the declarer has currently exposed during a Selling attempt.
  - No client ever receives the identity of another player's private hand cards, of cards no longer in play, or of cards that were once visible but are no longer visible to that viewer.
- **FR-023**: **No retained identities of cards that have left the viewer's visibility.** When a card transitions out of a viewer's visible scope (e.g., a talon card is absorbed into the declarer's hand; a Selling-exposed card returns to the declarer's hand on retry or moves into the buyer's hand on sale; in future phases, a played card from a completed trick), the server MUST stop sending that card's identity to that viewer, and the client MUST discard any previously-received identity for it. Cards that have already been "laid down" or otherwise left the viewer's view MUST NOT be retrievable from the client's state — the backend remains the only place that retains the full history.
- **FR-024**: Every visible movement of a card between locations (deck → hand, deck → talon, talon → declarer's hand, declarer's hand → center during Selling, center → buyer's hand on sale, center → declarer's hand on retry) MUST be presented as an animated motion on every client — cards MUST NOT snap to a new location instantaneously. State-changing actions MUST NOT be accepted by the client until the corresponding animation has completed (e.g., bidding controls are disabled until the deal animation finishes; the declarer's Sell/Start controls are disabled until the talon-absorption animation finishes). Cards that the viewer is not authorized to see (per FR-022) MUST animate as card-backs regardless of motion path.
- **FR-025**: **Persistent game-status display.** Every client MUST, at all times during an `in-progress` round, render a visible status area that communicates the current state of the round. This area MUST be a **fixed top bar spanning the full width above the table**, kept clear of the table/talon/hand regions so it never overlaps card content or the deal animation. It MUST be present from the moment the game screen appears (including during the deal animation) until the round ends (`play-phase-ready` or `aborted`), and MUST update within 1 second of any state change on the server. At minimum it MUST show:
  - **Current phase**, expressed in player-facing language: `Dealing`, `Bidding`, `Declarer deciding` (post-bid-decision), `Selling`, `Round ready to play`, or `Round aborted`.
  - **Whose turn it is**, identified by player nickname and seat position, for any phase where exactly one player is expected to act (Bidding, Declarer deciding, Selling). The viewer's own turn MUST be distinguishable from an opponent's turn (e.g., "Your turn" vs. "Waiting for {nickname}…").
  - **Current high bid** (integer) once bidding has produced at least one accepted bid; before then, the opening minimum (100) MUST be shown as the target to beat.
  - **Declarer identity** once determined (from the moment Bidding resolves through the end of the round).
  - **Selling attempt counter** during Selling and the post-bid-decision state that follows a failed Selling attempt — shown as "Attempt N of 3" with N ∈ {1, 2, 3}. The counter MUST disappear from view in phases where it does not apply (Dealing, Bidding, `play-phase-ready`, `aborted`).
  - **Passed-players indicator** during Bidding and Selling — the status area MUST make it clear which players have already passed in the current bidding/selling round, so the viewer can see at a glance who is still live.
  - **Disconnected-players indicator** at every phase — the status area (or the per-seat label) MUST show a "Disconnected…" marker next to any player currently within their grace period, and MUST remove the marker the instant they reconnect.
  The status display MUST be consistent across all three clients (the same phase, same active player, same high bid, same declarer, same attempt counter) — modulo the viewer-specific "Your turn" framing. The status display MUST NOT reveal any card identity that FR-022 forbids the viewer from seeing.
- **FR-026**: **Action gating by phase and turn.** On every client, an action control (button, card-selection affordance, input field, or any other interactive element that produces a state-changing message to the server) MUST be operable for the viewer only when **both** of these are true: (a) the round is in the phase in which that action applies, and (b) the viewer is the player whose turn it is to perform that action. When a control is not operable for the viewer, the client MUST choose between two presentations using this single rule:
  - **Disabled** (rendered, unmistakably greyed-out, and non-clickable) when the viewer is **eligible-but-waiting** — i.e., the viewer will, or could, regain that same control later in the same phase as the turn rotates, AND when a control conceptually exists in this phase but is temporarily unavailable to the player who would otherwise hold it (e.g., the Sell the Bid button after 3 failed attempts per FR-018; any control during an in-flight animation per FR-024).
  - **Hidden** (not rendered at all) when the control will **never** apply to this viewer for the remainder of the current phase — i.e., the viewer is permanently locked out of that action for this phase (e.g., a player who has already passed in Bidding cannot bid again; opponents during Declarer deciding never see Sell/Start).

  The server is the source of truth: even if a client renders an action it should not (because of bugs, dev-tools tampering, or stale state), the server MUST reject any state-changing message that does not satisfy (a) AND (b), with a user-visible reason, and MUST NOT advance the turn. Concrete per-phase mapping:
  - During **Dealing** (animation in progress): no player action controls exist for any viewer; controls that will appear in Bidding are **disabled** on the active bidder's client until the animation completes (per FR-024), and hidden on all other clients (they will never be operable there in Bidding).
  - During **Bidding**: only the active bidder's client shows operable Bid and Pass controls. Other still-eligible (not-yet-passed) bidders see Bid/Pass **disabled** until the turn rotates to them. Players who have already passed see Bid/Pass **hidden** (they will never act again in this Bidding phase).
  - During **Declarer deciding**: only the declarer's client shows operable Sell the Bid and Start the Game controls; opponents see Sell/Start **hidden** (they will never operate them in this phase). If the declarer is on attempt 3 and has failed Selling already (per FR-018), Sell the Bid is **disabled** on the declarer's client; Start the Game remains operable.
  - During **Selling — card-selection step**: only the declarer's client renders the 3-card-toggle affordance on their own hand and the **Sell** / **Cancel** buttons (per FR-029); opponents see no card-selection affordance and no Sell/Cancel buttons (**hidden** for opponents — they will never select). The **Sell** confirm button is **disabled** on the declarer's client until exactly 3 cards are selected.
  - During **Selling — opponent-bid step**: only the not-yet-passed non-declarer opponent whose turn it is sees operable Bid/Pass. The other not-yet-passed opponent (the one waiting their turn) sees Bid/Pass **disabled**. An opponent who has already passed in this selling attempt sees Bid/Pass **hidden** (locked out for the rest of this attempt). The original declarer sees Bid/Pass **hidden** throughout the Selling opponent-bid step (they are permanently excluded from buying their own bid per FR-015).
  - During **Round ready to play**: no game-action controls (Bid, Pass, Sell, Start, card selection) are rendered on any client (**hidden** — none will ever operate in this phase); the only operable control is the **Back to Lobby** button per FR-019.
  - During **Round aborted**: no player action controls from this spec's scope are rendered on any client (**hidden** — the abort flow returns players to the lobby automatically per FR-021).
- **FR-027**: **Reconnect rehydration is a snapshot, not a replay.** When a player reconnects within their grace period, the server MUST send a single state snapshot containing only the card identities that are currently visible to that viewer per FR-022 (their own hand, plus the talon or Selling-exposed cards if applicable) together with the full view-model that drives the persistent status area per FR-025 (phase, active player, current high bid, declarer, passed players, disconnected players, selling attempt counter). The client MUST render the resulting layout immediately, with no animation, regardless of how many card movements the player missed while disconnected. Card movements that occur *after* reconnect MUST animate normally per FR-024. The server MUST NOT send any card identity that has left the viewer's visible scope (per FR-023), even though that identity was previously sent before disconnect — the reconnecting client receives the *current* visible state only, not its history.
- **FR-028**: **Bid input mechanism.** The active bidder's client MUST present the bid amount via a single numeric field that the player can either (a) edit directly by typing, or (b) adjust via `−5` and `+5` stepper buttons attached to the field. The field MUST initialize each time it appears for the active bidder to the smallest legal bid for that moment (i.e., `max(100, currentHighBid + 5)`). The stepper MUST clamp its output to the inclusive range `[max(100, currentHighBid + 5), 300]` and MUST move in increments of 5 so its output is always a legal multiple. Typed input MUST NOT be clamped or auto-corrected (the player may enter any integer, including invalid ones), but the **Bid** submit button MUST be disabled whenever the current field value fails any of the rules in FR-008 (so an invalid value cannot be submitted from the UI). A separate **Pass** button MUST always be operable for the active bidder alongside the bid field. A single click on **Pass** commits immediately with no confirmation step; the player is locked out of further bidding for the round (FR-009) or for the current selling attempt (FR-015) from that moment on. Regardless of what the client does, the server MUST re-validate every submitted bid per FR-008 and reject invalid ones with a user-visible reason per FR-026. This same input mechanism MUST be used during Selling for the two non-declarer opponents on their turn, with the initial value set to `currentHighBid + 5` of the original bid that opened Selling.
- **FR-029**: **Selling card-selection mechanism.** When the declarer enters the Selling card-selection step, their client MUST allow them to toggle individual cards in their 10-card hand by tapping/clicking: a tap on an unselected card marks it as selected (with an unmistakable visual highlight); a tap on a selected card deselects it. The client MUST visibly track how many of the required 3 are currently selected (e.g., "Selected: 2 / 3"). A **Sell** confirm button MUST be operable only when exactly 3 cards are selected — fewer or more MUST keep it disabled. A **Cancel** button MUST always be operable during selection and MUST return the declarer to the post-bid decision state (Sell / Start) without consuming a selling attempt. Submitting **Sell** sends the 3 selected card identifiers to the server; the server MUST verify (a) all 3 are currently in the declarer's hand, (b) all 3 are distinct, and (c) the set differs from every prior selling attempt in this round per FR-016, rejecting otherwise with a user-visible reason per FR-026. Opponents' clients during this step MUST NOT render any card-selection affordance on the declarer's hand (which they see as face-down anyway per FR-022).
- **FR-030**: **Per-player soft rate limit on game actions.** The server MUST apply a per-player throttle on state-changing in-round messages (Bid, Pass, Sell-select-submit, Start, Cancel, and any other action that mutates round state). At most one such message per player MUST be processed every 250 ms; messages arriving from the same player within an active window MUST be silently dropped on the server with no broadcast and no acknowledgement (so the offending client receives no feedback that it was throttled). This throttle is independent of, and additional to, the per-action validation in FR-008 and FR-026 and the idempotent handling of duplicate Sell/Start clicks; it is intended to protect the room from a single misbehaving or runaway client. The throttle window is short enough to be invisible during normal human play.
- **FR-031**: **Rejection feedback surface.** Every server-rejected action that produces a "user-visible reason" (per FR-008, FR-014, FR-021, FR-026, FR-028, FR-029) MUST be surfaced on the rejecting player's client as a non-blocking toast notification reusing the existing `Toast` overlay pattern (a short banner that auto-dismisses after a few seconds and does not require acknowledgement). The toast MUST contain a concise human-readable explanation of the rejection (e.g., "Bid must be a multiple of 5", "Card no longer in your hand"). The rejection toast MUST be sent only to the rejecting player — never broadcast to the other two players' clients. Messages that are silently dropped by the per-player throttle (FR-030) MUST NOT produce a toast on any client.
- **FR-032**: **Game record cleanup on round end.** When the server emits the `play-phase-ready` event (per FR-019) or broadcasts `round_aborted` (per FR-021), the game record MUST be purged from the server's in-memory store as part of the same operation. The post-round "Round ready to play — next phase coming soon" screen (FR-019) is then maintained purely as client-side state on each player's browser; the **Back to Lobby** button is purely client-side navigation that does not require the server-side game record to still exist. A player who disconnects after `play-phase-ready` or `round_aborted` has been emitted MUST be routed directly to the lobby on reconnect, since the game record no longer exists to rehydrate (FR-027 only applies while the round is still active).

### Key Entities

- **Round**: a single play of the 24-card deck. Tracks dealer seat, current phase (`bidding | post-bid-decision | selling | play-phase-ready | aborted`), current declarer, current high bid, list of players who have passed, and the declarer's selling-attempt counter (0–3).
- **Hand**: a player's private set of cards. Size is 7 most of the time, temporarily 10 for the declarer between absorbing the talon and either starting the game or selling, and temporarily 7 for the declarer during a selling attempt (3 cards exposed face-up).
- **Talon**: the 3 cards dealt to the center. Face-up to all players until the declarer absorbs them, after which the talon area is empty.
- **Bid**: an action with `playerId`, `amount` (integer ≥ 100, multiple of 5, ≤ 300), and an implicit ordering. A Pass is a separate kind of action (`amount = null`).
- **Sell Attempt**: an ordered entry within Selling, capturing the 3 exposed card identifiers and the outcome (`sold-to: playerId` or `all-passed`). Up to 3 per round. The full Sell Attempt history (including which card identities were exposed in past attempts) is retained only on the server — clients never receive it after the cards have left their view (see FR-022, FR-023).
- **Card**: rank ∈ {9, 10, J, Q, K, A}, suit ∈ {♥, ♦, ♣, ♠}. Point values exist in the rulebook but are not used in this spec (deferred to scoring).
- **Game Status (view-model)**: the per-client projection that drives the persistent status display (FR-025). Fields: `phase` (one of `Dealing | Bidding | Declarer deciding | Selling | Round ready to play | Round aborted`), `activePlayer` (nickname + seat, or `null` when no one is expected to act), `viewerIsActive` (boolean — used to render "Your turn" vs. "Waiting for…"), `currentHighBid` (integer; the opening minimum 100 before any bid is accepted), `declarer` (nickname, or `null` until bidding resolves), `passedPlayers` (list of nicknames who have passed in the current bidding or selling round), and `sellAttempt` (integer 1–3 during Selling and the post-bid-decision state that follows a failed attempt; `null` otherwise). The view-model holds no card identities and is therefore safe to render on all three clients identically.

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

### Measurable Outcomes

- **SC-001**: The game starts within 2 seconds of the 3rd player joining the waiting room (all three browsers show the game screen).
- **SC-002**: Across 100 rounds, each player receives exactly 7 cards and the talon contains exactly 3 cards at deal time, with zero duplicate cards across all hands and talon (100% correctness).
- **SC-003**: 100% of invalid bid attempts (wrong divisibility, out of range, not strictly increasing) are rejected with a user-visible reason and do not advance the turn.
- **SC-004**: At the end of bidding there is always exactly one declarer with a bid in [100, 300] — never zero, never two.
- **SC-005**: When the round transitions to play-phase-ready (by Start the Game), the finalized declarer holds exactly 10 cards, each opponent holds exactly 7, and the talon area is empty.
- **SC-006**: Across all states in this spec, no client ever holds the identity (rank/suit) of any card that is not currently visible to that viewer — including cards in opponents' private hands, talon cards after the declarer has absorbed them, Selling-exposed cards after they have been returned or sold, and any card "laid down" or otherwise out of the viewer's view. A network capture of the server→client traffic confirms identities are sent only at the moment of visibility and never re-sent or retained for cards that have left view.
- **SC-007**: Selling concludes within a bounded number of attempts: a declarer can never make more than 3 selling attempts in one round.
- **SC-008**: At every moment from game-screen appearance to round end, 100% of clients display the current phase and (when applicable) the active player, the current high bid, the declarer, and the selling-attempt counter; the displayed status reflects any server state change within 1 second.
- **SC-009**: 100% of action attempts that violate phase-or-turn gating — whether the control was rendered (a client bug) or the message was forged (a tampering client) — are rejected by the server with a user-visible reason and do not change round state. Across normal play, action controls appear operable on a client only when that client's viewer is both in the right phase AND is the player whose turn it is.

## Assumptions

- **Single round only**: this spec covers exactly one round of bidding and selling. Multi-round play (dealer rotation, cumulative score, barrel rule, three-zeros rule, victory at 1000) is **out of scope** for this spec.
- **3-player mode only**: 4-player support is a future feature; the current store already defaults `requiredPlayers = 3`.
- **Dealer is the game host** for the single round defined here.
- **No turn timer for v1**: players take as long as they need. Disconnect handling relies on feature 003's grace period; expiry aborts the round.
- **Marriages, trump declaration, card exchange (passing 2 cards), trick play, and scoring are out of scope** for this spec — they belong to the follow-up feature triggered by the "play phase ready" handoff.
- **In-memory persistence**: a server restart aborts any in-flight round (consistent with the existing `ThousandStore`).
- **Layout adaptation**: the layout sketch (`docs/sketches/game-layout.png`) was drawn for 4 players; for 3-player mode the "P4 cards face down" area at the top is omitted. The 3 player seats are: self (bottom), opponent left, opponent right. Other reserved areas (leaderboard, chat) keep their existing lobby behavior — unchanged by this spec.
- **Visual/styling details** (exact pixel sizes, easing curves, durations, card art) are decided at planning/implementation time, not in this spec. The spec enforces the **information layout** and the **requirement that card movements are animated** (FR-023); the precise animation timings and styling are left to implementation.
- **Accessibility / colorblind support is out of scope for this spec.** Non-color suit signaling, a high-contrast mode, and similar accessibility affordances are deferred to a future user-settings feature (toggled from a settings menu). This spec does not mandate any colorblind-safe rendering for cards or the selected-card highlight.

## Clarifications

### Session 2026-05-11

- Q: What do non-declarers see for the 3 talon cards during bidding? → A: The 3 talon cards are dealt face-up and visible to all 3 players from the deal until the declarer absorbs them. Once absorbed, they become part of the declarer's private hand (face-down to opponents).
- Q: How is the dealer chosen for the single round? → A: The game's host (the player who created the game) is the dealer. The first bidder is the player clockwise-left of the host.
- Q: Should there be a turn timer for bidding or selling actions? → A: No timer for v1. Players take as long as they need; only the grace-period purge on disconnect (feature 003) protects against an absent player blocking the round.
- Q: When a player disconnects mid-round, does the active turn pause regardless of whose turn it is, or only when the disconnected player is the active player? → A: Only the active player's disconnect pauses the turn; non-active disconnects let play continue for the rest. In both cases, every client shows a "Disconnected…" indicator next to the disconnected player's seat until they reconnect or the grace period expires.
- Q: What does the user see after the "play phase ready" handoff (since the play phase itself is out of scope)? → A: All three clients show a "Round ready to play — next phase coming soon" message and a single operable "Back to Lobby" button; each player returns to the lobby individually when they press it.
- Q: On reconnect within the grace period, does the client replay missed card-movement animations or snap to current state? → A: Snap to current state. The server sends a single visible-state snapshot (per FR-022) plus the view-model; the client renders the layout immediately with no animation. Movements after reconnect animate normally.
- Q: How does the active bidder enter their bid amount? → A: A numeric field that supports both direct typing AND `−5`/`+5` stepper buttons. Field initializes to `max(100, currentHighBid + 5)`; stepper clamps to `[max(100, currentHighBid + 5), 300]`; typing is unrestricted but the Bid button is disabled when the current value is invalid. Pass is a separate always-operable button for the active bidder. Server re-validates every submission.
- Q: How does the declarer pick the 3 cards to expose during Selling? → A: Click/tap-to-toggle on cards in the declarer's hand; a "Sell" confirm button enables only when exactly 3 are selected; a "Cancel" button returns to the post-bid decision state without consuming an attempt. Server verifies the set is in-hand, distinct, and different from every prior attempt this round.
- Q: Which opponent appears on the viewer's left vs right? → A: The opponent who acts immediately after the viewer in clockwise turn order is rendered on the viewer's LEFT; the other opponent is rendered on the viewer's RIGHT. Applied consistently on every client.
- Q: Where on the screen does the persistent status area live? → A: A fixed top bar spanning the full width above the table, kept clear of the table/talon/hand regions so it never overlaps card content or the deal animation.
- Q: In what order are the viewer's own hand cards arranged? → A: Auto-sorted by suit, then by rank within suit (ascending 9, 10, J, Q, K, A). Re-sorted whenever the hand contents change. The viewer cannot manually reorder. **Suit order is descending by trump/marriage value (from the rulebook): ♣ Clubs (100) → ♠ Spades (80) → ♥ Hearts (60) → ♦ Diamonds (40), left to right.**
- Q: Colorblind / non-color suit signaling and selection highlight? → A: Deferred — will be added later via a user-settings feature. Out of scope for this spec.
- Q: Server-side rate limiting for in-round actions? → A: Per-player soft throttle of one state-changing message per 250 ms; excess silently dropped server-side, with no broadcast and no acknowledgement. Additional to per-action validation and Sell/Start idempotency.
- Q: How are server rejection reasons (invalid bid, invalid card selection, out-of-turn action, disconnect-blocked action, etc.) surfaced to the rejecting player? → A: Non-blocking toast via the existing `Toast` overlay (~4 s auto-dismiss). Sent only to the rejecting player — never broadcast. Silently-throttled drops (FR-030) produce no toast.
- Q: Does pressing the Pass button (in Bidding or Selling) require a confirmation step? → A: No — a single click commits immediately, no confirmation modal or two-tap. The labeled button captures intent; the player is responsible.
- Q: When does the server remove the game record from `ThousandStore` after the round ends? → A: Immediately upon emitting `play-phase-ready` (FR-019) or `round_aborted` (FR-021). The post-round screen is purely client-side; Back to Lobby is pure client navigation; reconnects after this point go directly to the lobby.
- Q: Is there an explicit "Leave Game" button rendered on the game screen during in-progress phases? → A: No. Players who wish to leave mid-round close the browser tab; this is treated as a passive disconnect, and the standard FR-021 grace-period flow applies (round aborts if they do not reconnect in time).
- Q: During Selling, do the two opponents get just one Bid/Pass chance each, or does bidding rotate between them until one passes? → A: Rotate clockwise like main bidding — each opponent on their turn bids (≥ currentHigh + 5, ≤ 300) or passes; bidding continues among the non-passed opponents until exactly one opponent remains non-passed (they win the buy) or both pass on their first turns (attempt fails). Passes during a selling attempt do **not** persist across attempts — eligibility resets at the next attempt.
- Q: Which opponent gets the first chance to buy on each selling attempt? → A: The seat clockwise-left of the declarer goes first; the other opponent goes second. Parallels FR-004 (main bidding starts clockwise-left of the dealer) and matches the FR-005 seating rule (the opponent who acts next is always rendered on the viewer's left).
- Q: In the deal animation, when in the 24-card sequence are the 3 talon cards dealt? → A: Interleaved — rounds 1, 2, and 3 each deal P1 → P2 → P3 → Talon (12 cards, after which everyone has 3 cards and the talon has 3); rounds 4–7 each deal P1 → P2 → P3 only (12 more cards) to fill hands to 7. P1 is the seat clockwise-left of the dealer (the first bidder per FR-004). All clients animate the same canonical 24-step sequence.
- Q: For controls that are not operable for the viewer (FR-026), when should they be **hidden** vs **disabled**? → A: **Disabled** when the viewer is eligible-but-waiting (the same control will, or could, become operable for them later in the same phase as the turn rotates — e.g., the other still-eligible bidder during Bidding, the not-yet-passed waiting opponent during the Selling opponent-bid step, Sell-the-Bid after 3 failed attempts, any control during an in-flight animation). **Hidden** when the viewer is permanently locked out of that action for the remainder of the current phase (e.g., a player who has already passed in Bidding, opponents during Declarer deciding, opponents during the Selling card-selection step, the original declarer during the Selling opponent-bid step, all game-action controls during Round ready to play / Round aborted).
